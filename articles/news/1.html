Humanitarian crisis worsens in Gaza as Israel-Hamas war intensifies

Israel continues to battle Hamas in several parts of Gaza, including in the main southern city of Khan Younis, where residents have been urged to evacuate to an area with few facilities on the coast. Israel's military said Sunday it struck more than 250 Hamas targets in the past 24 hours, including a military communications site.

Israel and Hamas are not not showing the "same willingness" as before last month’s week-long truce to resolve the war, Qatar's prime minister, a key negotiator, said Sunday, while also condemning the humanitarian situation in the enclave. His comments came as the UN secretary general said he "will not give up" calling for a ceasefire in Gaza after the Secretary Council failed to pass a resolution to do so.

Israel's national security adviser said Israel has so far killed 7,000 Hamas fighters, calling it a "minimal estimate." But the Palestinian prime minister said Israel's goal of destroying Hamas was "not going to happen."

WHO's chief called the impact of the conflict on the health care system in Gaza "catastrophic" as fighting rages on. More than 17,500 people have been killed since the conflict began, the Hamas-run ministry of health in Gaza says.

But finding essential food and drinkable water remains a major challenge, he told CNN on a phone call.

The price of basic items like flour has gone up eight-fold since the start of the conflict, and cooking gas, in particular, is in very short supply, he said.

About Khan Younis: The city is located in the southern part of the Gaza strip. It was where thousands of Palestinians fled to earlier in the war when Israel called for an evacuation of northern Gaza and directed residents southwards. Now, on Saturday, Israel issued another “urgent appeal” for evacuation — this time asking civilians of Khan Younis to move to an area on the coast, which has few facilities. It is unclear how many people were aware of the instruction given the lack of communications networks and internet availability in much of Gaza.
